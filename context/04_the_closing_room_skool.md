# The Closing Room: Skool Community (Skool Fallback Route)

## What it is

A free Skool community at `community.systemiseandsell.com/join`. Cloudflare geo-gated to qualified geographies (UK, US, CA, AU, NZ, EU). Members get full access to:

- 16-module $200M Closing Course (the same content as the first two-thirds of the paid programme)
- Daily live calls with Marco Fernandes, Mon-Fri, 7pm UK / 2pm EST / 11am PT
- Live calls archive and replays
- Hiring Partners reference course (3 pages: how placement works, who's hiring, interview process)
- Resources, chat, and posting (chat unlocks at Skool Level 2)

## What it is FREE

Completely free. No credit card. No upsell inside the community. No "premium tier." This is genuine.

The audience is sophisticated, many have been burned by upsell ladders. Skool is positioned as a no-strings way to audition Marco's training before any sales conversation.

## How the Skool fallback works in this bot

The Skool community is the **save mechanism** when a prospect declines the paid call.

**Flow:**

1. Setter pushes the discovery call (Push to Call)
2. Prospect says "I can't afford it right now" / "the investment isn't possible" / "I'm not in a position to pay yet"
3. Setter triggers the Skool Fallback response
4. Bot generates a message that pivots cleanly: "no problem, come into the free community first, audition the training, no charge, the path to placement starts here"
5. Prospect joins Skool
6. Over 30-90 days, community engagement signals warm them up
7. Setter re-approaches with Skool Re-engagement (Template 6 style)
8. Booked call → close

## When the Skool fallback IS deployed

The Skool route is only triggered when the prospect's objection is **investment / money / timing**. Examples that should trigger the fallback:

- "I can't afford that right now"
- "The investment isn't possible for me at the moment"
- "I'm interested but money is tight"
- "I'd love to but I'm not in a position to pay yet"
- "Maybe later when I have the funds"
- "I'd need to save up first"

## When the Skool fallback is NOT deployed

The Skool route is **never** used as a consolation prize for prospects who aren't a fit. Examples that should NOT trigger Skool:

- "Not interested"
- "This isn't for me"
- "I'm not looking for sales training"
- "I changed my mind"
- "I don't think it's a fit"

For those, give a clean exit: "All good, no worries. If anything changes down the line, the door's open." Then stop. Don't push Skool. Pushing Skool on someone who said "not interested" is desperate and damages brand trust.

The setter decides which path to take. The bot generates the message based on the setter's choice.

## How a Skool fallback message should feel

The tone: respectful of the no, no arguing, no pressure, genuinely positions Skool as a no-strings option.

**Three things every Skool fallback message must do:**

1. Acknowledge the no without trying to talk them out of it
2. Reframe the Skool community as a no-pressure way to audition Marco and the methodology, with the same training inside
3. Make it crystal clear it's free, with no upsell or upgrade pressure inside

**Three things a Skool fallback message must NEVER do:**

1. Argue with the objection (never "I get it, but…")
2. Imply they can "upgrade later", the Skool community is its own thing, not a downsell
3. Apply any pressure or scarcity ("but only for this week", never)

## How a Skool re-engagement message should feel

When a Skool member has hit qualification signals (completed Modules 1-3, attended 3+ lives, viewed Hiring Partners pages, posting in chat), the setter triggers a Skool Re-engagement message.

The tone: notice their engagement, ask a qualifying question, frame the paid programme as the **next** step, never the **upsell**.

**Template 6 reference (Tiago's actual message that's been booking calls):**

> "Hey [name], saw you've worked through the first three modules, that's the foundations sorted.
>
> Quick question: are you closing already, or working towards it?
>
> Reason I ask: we run a separate placement programme that gets graduates of our paid training into interviews with vetted hiring partners (15% commission, 30-day guarantee). It's not for everyone, it's specifically for people who want to be working as a high-ticket closer for one of our partner companies.
>
> If that sounds like you, happy to set up a call with our team to walk you through it. If not, no worries, just keep working through the course."

This template does three jobs:

1. Acknowledges engagement (validation)
2. Asks a qualifying question (closing already, or working towards)
3. Frames the paid programme as separate (no bait-and-switch, the community is the community, the programme is the programme)

The bot's Skool Re-engagement examples should follow this shape exactly.
