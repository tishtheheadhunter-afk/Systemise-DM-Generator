# General Objection Handle, Examples

**When to use:** The prospect has raised an objection that ISN'T about money/investment. Things like:

- "I've done programmes like this before and they didn't work"
- "How do I know this isn't another scam?"
- "I don't have time for weekly live sessions"
- "What if I'm not good enough for the placement?"
- "I need to think about it"
- "I want to research more before committing"

For money/investment objections specifically, use Skool Fallback (file 08) instead.

**Goal:** Validate the objection (never dismiss), reframe with substance, then redirect to the call (or Skool, if it makes sense).

**Generation count:** 3 options.

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Prospect said: "I've bought 2 closer training programmes before and never got placed. Why is this different?"

### Option A (Validate + name the gap)

> Fair question [name], and honestly, the skepticism is the right instinct 👌 most closer training programmes are video libraries, you watch, you don't apply, nothing changes.
>
> The differentiation here is structural. Weekly live training with me where we actually unpack scenarios, real roleplays with feedback, call breakdowns on real campaigns, and guaranteed interview opportunities once you certify ✅ it's not training plus hope. It's training plus access.
>
> Worth a call to dig into what specifically didn't work in the previous ones and whether this would actually solve it. Mind if I send the link? 😊

### Option B (Direct comparison)

> Fair concern [name] 👌
>
> Honest answer, most "closer training" is theory, no application, no path to a real campaign. People finish, feel skilled-up, then realise they've got nowhere to apply it.
>
> Structurally, what's different. I run live training weekly, we do real roleplays with feedback, and there are guaranteed interview opportunities at the end with vetted partners ✅ it's the application and the access that the other programmes were missing.
>
> Worth getting on a call so you can grill us on the specifics and figure out if it's actually different. Want me to set it up? 😊

### Option C (Reframe + qualify)

> [Name], the skepticism is fair 👌 I'd ask the same question.
>
> Quick question before I send a long answer, when the previous programmes failed for you, was it because the training was bad, or because there was no real path to a campaign at the end? 🎯
>
> Knowing that tells me whether this would actually solve your problem or whether you'd be better off elsewhere.

**Notes on what works:**

- All three start by validating the skepticism, never dismiss it as a "limiting belief"
- All three name the specific gap in most programmes (theory without application, training without access)
- Option A and B redirect to the call, useful when the prospect is high-intent
- Option C asks a clarifying question instead of redirecting immediately, useful when the prospect needs to feel heard before being moved forward
- None of them attack the previous programmes by name, keeps Marco's brand professional

> Fair point [name] 👌 the space has earned its bad reputation.
>

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Validate first.** "Fair question" / "Honest concern" / "I'd ask the same", never dismiss.
2. **Name the gap or reframe.** Why is this different? What were the other programmes missing?
3. **Redirect to the call (or Skool, where appropriate).** The objection is best resolved on a real conversation, not in DMs.
4. **Drop the link inline.** Reduces friction. Don't make them ask.
5. **Easy opt-out.** "When you're ready" / "no pressure", protects the relationship.

## What to avoid

- "I understand your concern but…", the "but" cancels the validation
- Defensive responses, confident programmes don't need to defend
- Hard closes after an objection ("you have to decide now"), pressure
- Comparing/attacking specific competitor programmes by name
- Long monologues, keep responses tight, redirect to call quickly
- Pricing details (still no pricing in DMs even when objection is "what's the cost"), redirect to call
