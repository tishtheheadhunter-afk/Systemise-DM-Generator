# Follow-up, Examples

**When to use:** The prospect has gone quiet. They were engaged before, now they're not replying. We're nudging them back into the conversation.

**Goal:** Light, warm, never pressuring. Re-engage without making them feel chased.

**Generation count:** Cadence ladder (the bot generates the right one for the right point in the ladder, based on what stage of follow-up we're at).

The Sir Marco template's follow-up cadence works well and we adapt it here. Key adaptation: Marco Fernandes' voice is direct, so the follow-ups are slightly less smiley than Sir Marco's setter team.

---

## Cadence rules (for the setter to know)

**If they've never responded to the first message:**
- Follow up every 24 hours
- Maximum 4 follow-ups before stopping

**If they responded but haven't been sent a link yet:**
- Follow up every 12 hours
- Maximum 6 follow-ups before stopping

**If they've been sent a booking link but haven't booked:**
- Follow up every 6 hours
- Continue until they book, ask to stop, or block

These match the Sir Marco template rules exactly. Cadence is consistent. The voice is what changes.

---

## Stage 1: Never responded to first message

### Follow-up 1 (24h after first message)

> Still of interest [name]? 🙂

### Follow-up 2 (24h later)

> Let me know either way 🙂

### Follow-up 3 (24h later)

> Everything okay [name]?

### Follow-up 4 (final, 24h later)

> Last nudge from me [name]. If now's not the time, no worries, door's always open if you change your mind 🙏

**Notes on what works:**

- Each follow-up gets shorter and lighter
- Follow-up 4 is the cutoff, explicitly says "last nudge", removes pressure
- "Door's always open" gives a clean exit
- No emojis after the first two, Marco's voice goes direct in follow-up 3 and 4

---

## Stage 2: Responded but not yet sent the link

These are warmer prospects who got into a conversation and then went quiet mid-arc.

### Follow-up 1 (12h after their last reply)

> Hey [name], following up on our convo, still keen to dig into this? 😊

### Follow-up 2 (12h later)

> [Name], if it's not the right time just let me know, no problem 🙂

### Follow-up 3 (12h later)

> Hey, just checking in [name] 👋 want to keep going or pause?

### Follow-up 4 (12h later)

> [Name], life can get busy 🙂 whenever you're ready to pick this back up, hit me back.

### Follow-up 5 (12h later, getting lighter)

> Just a nudge 🙂

### Follow-up 6 (final, 12h later)

> Last one from me [name], door's open whenever, no pressure either way 🙏

**Notes on what works:**

- Tone progresses from direct check-in → empathetic ("life gets busy") → very light ("just a nudge")
- Always offers the easy out ("if it's not the right time, no problem")
- Never accuses them of ghosting or guilts them
- Follow-up 6 is the cutoff with a graceful exit

---

## Stage 3: Sent the booking link, hasn't booked

This is where the Sir Marco template gets more persistent, every 6 hours until they book, block, or ask to stop. Marco Fernandes' voice keeps that cadence but stays light.

### Follow-up 1 (6h after link sent)

> Hey [name], did the link come through okay? 🙂

### Follow-up 2 (6h later)

> [Name], anything blocking you from picking a slot? 🙂

### Follow-up 3 (6h later)

> Hey [name], if there's a specific time that works, let me know and I can ask the team to make it work 😊

### Follow-up 4 (6h later)

> Just checking in [name] 🙂

### Follow-up 5 (6h later)

> [Name], I know things get busy, happy to send the link again if you've lost it 🙂 https://api.leadconnectorhq.com/widget/bookings/systemiseandselldiscoverycall

### Follow-up 6 (6h later)

> Hey [name]? 🙂

### Follow-up 7+ (continue every 6h)

Continue with light, varied nudges. Examples:

> Quick check-in [name] 🙂

> [Name], all good your end? 🙂

> Hey [name], any update? 🙂

Until they book, block, or explicitly ask to stop.

**Notes on what works:**

- Stage 3 is the most active because the prospect has already said yes, they're likely just busy or distracted
- Follow-ups stay short and light, never "you said you'd book, where are you?"
- Re-sending the link in Follow-up 5 is genuinely helpful (people lose links)
- The cadence is per Sir Marco's template, proven to work
- Stop only when they book, block, or explicitly say "stop messaging me"

## Special-case follow-ups

### After a Skool fallback (no specific cadence, light)

If the prospect went into Skool and you want to follow up later (4-6 weeks):

> Hey [name], saw you've been in the community for a few weeks now 👋 how's it going? Anything specific I can help with? 😊

### After they said "let me think about it" (light, 3-5 days later)

> Hey [name], no pressure, just checking if anything came up from your thinking that I can help with 🙂

### After they declined entirely (one final check-in, 30 days later)

> Hey [name], been a while 👋 any change in where you're at? If not, all good. If yes, you know where to find me.

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Short.** Follow-ups are not the place for monologues.
2. **Always offer the easy out.** "If it's not the right time, no problem."
3. **Never guilt or accuse.** "I haven't heard back" without the negative spin.
4. **One soft emoji per follow-up.** 🙂 / 😊 / 🙏 / 👋 are the workhorses. Never stacked. Follow-ups stay light.
5. **Resend the link if they've lost it.** Practical helpfulness over passive-aggression.

## What to avoid

- "I notice you haven't replied", accusatory
- "Just bumping this to the top", corporate spam-speak
- "Did you see my last message?", passive-aggressive
- Long re-pitches of the offer, they remember
- "Last chance!" / "Spots are filling!", false urgency
- Stacked emojis ("👋👋👋"), desperate. Hype emojis (🔥 💯 🚀), wrong voice.
- More than 4 / 6 / continuous follow-ups in their respective stages without a stop signal
