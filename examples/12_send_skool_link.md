# Send Skool Link, Examples

**When to use:** The prospect has explicitly asked for the Skool community link, OR after a Skool fallback message they've said "yes, send me the link." This is the mechanical action of delivering the URL cleanly.

**Goal:** Send the link, set the expectation for what to do next, exit gracefully.

**Generation count:** 1 option (mechanical).

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Prospect just said: "Yeah send me the Skool link."

### Option

> Here you go [name] 👌
>
> https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> Once you're in, the course is in the Classroom and I run lives Mon to Fri. Drop a hello in the chat when you join so we know you're there 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- Direct delivery, no preamble
- One sentence of "what to do next", points them to the course and the daily lives
- "Drop a hello in the chat" is a soft engagement nudge, gets them to Level 2 in Skool, which unlocks more of the course
- One emoji at the end softens the sign-off without being excessive

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> After a Skool fallback message, prospect replied: "Sounds good, give me the link."

### Option

> Here it is 🙂
>
> https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> Course is open in the Classroom, lives Mon to Fri at 7pm UK / 2pm EST / 11am PT. Take your time working through the foundations and post in the chat anytime, happy to answer questions as you go.

**Notes on what works:**

- Adds the live times, useful, removes a barrier ("when do I show up?")
- Encourages chat posting, engagement signal that triggers later qualification
- "Happy to answer questions", opens the door for ongoing conversation, doesn't close it after the link delivery

## Example 3

**Setter input:**
> Prospect spontaneously asked: "Do you have a free option I can check out first before paying for anything?"

### Option

> Yeah we do [name] 🙂
>
> https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> Free Skool community, same first two-thirds of the training course inside, daily lives with me Mon to Fri, no upsell, no pressure ✅ most people use it to audition the methodology before any paid commitment.
>
> Take a look. If it clicks, you'll know.

**Notes on what works:**

- The prospect asked unprompted, bot can be a bit more informational about what they're getting
- "Same first two-thirds of the training", accurate framing, builds trust
- "No upsell, no pressure", explicit, since the prospect is testing whether this is a downsell trap
- "Take a look. If it clicks, you'll know.", confident, low-pressure exit

---

## What to avoid

- Restating the entire offer before sending the link, they asked for the link
- Adding pressure ("but the paid programme is where the real magic happens")
- Implying Skool is "just a taste" of the paid programme, wrong framing; it's its own legitimate path
- Sending the wrong link (the only correct Skool URL is `https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join`)
- Hype emojis 🔥 💯 🚀 or stacked emojis. 1-2 grounded emojis (😊 🙂 ✅) is the standard.
