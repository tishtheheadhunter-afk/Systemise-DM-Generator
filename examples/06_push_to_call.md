# Push to Call, Examples

**When to use:** We've qualified the prospect through Steps 2-4 of the qualification arc. They've shared their situation, their why, and (ideally) their goal. Now we're at Step 5: bridging to the discovery call.

**Goal:** Frame the call as a deeper conversation with the team, not a sales pitch. Get the prospect to agree to a booking link.

**Generation count:** 3 options.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Prospect has shared: 5 years in B2B SaaS sales, currently at $80k OTE, hates the corporate structure, wants remote freedom, has been watching Marco's content for 3 months, mentioned they want to be "fully remote and uncapped" within 6 months.

### Option A (Direct bridge, references their goals)

> Based on everything you've shared [name], I think it's worth taking this further ✅
>
> Mind if I get you a call with our team? They'll dig into your sales background and your goal of going fully remote and uncapped, walk you through how the programme works specifically for someone moving from B2B SaaS, and confirm whether it's the right fit from both sides 🤝
>
> Sound good? 😊

### Option B (Brief feedback + bridge)

> [Name], you've got the background (5 years in B2B SaaS) and the clarity on what you want (remote, uncapped, 6 months). That's most of what we look for ✅
>
> The next step is a call with our team, they'll go deeper on your specific situation, walk you through the placement side, and confirm fit from both sides. No pitch, just a real conversation 🤝
>
> Want me to set you up? 😊

### Option C (Authority frame + bridge)

> Honest take [name] 💡 you've got more advantages than you realise. 5 years selling B2B means you've already got the bones. Most people we work with start with less.
>
> Worth the call. Our team will get into your specific background, the path from B2B to high-ticket commission-only, and what the next 6 months actually look like 🎯
>
> Mind if I send you the link? 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- All three reference what the prospect specifically shared (5 years B2B, remote goal, 6-month timeline), shows the conversation has been heard
- Option A is the cleanest direct bridge, fits most situations
- Option B leads with "you've got the background", gives the prospect a confidence boost before the ask
- Option C uses Marco's signature framing ("more advantages than you realise"), works for prospects who seem unsure of themselves
- All three frame the call as "deeper conversation, fit from both sides", never as a sales pitch
- All three end with a soft yes/no question, not a hard close

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> Prospect has shared: never sold before, currently in retail, finds the idea of high-ticket closing intimidating but exciting, wants to know "if it's actually possible for someone like me."

### Option A (Reframe + bridge)

> [Name], "someone like me" is the audience we built this for ✅ most of our placed closers came in with zero sales background. Retail, hospitality, admin, customer service. The thing they had in common was a willingness to do the reps and let the training do the work.
>
> The next step is a call with our team. They'll dig into your specific situation, walk you through how someone with no sales background goes through the programme, and confirm whether it's the right fit. No pitch, real conversation 🤝
>
> Want me to set it up? 😊

### Option B (Address the doubt directly + bridge)

> [Name], the "is it possible for someone like me" question is the one almost every successful closer in our programme asked at the start 💡 the honest answer is yes, but only if you're willing to be coached and put the reps in. Talent doesn't matter; consistency and willingness to be wrong on calls until you're right does.
>
> Best way to find out if you're a fit is a call with our team. They'll get into your background, what you'd be walking into, and whether you'd actually thrive in the programme. Sound good? 😊

### Option C (Stories + bridge)

> [Name], the closer who I'm proudest of in the last cohort came in from retail. Six months later they're closing $5k+ months on a high-ticket coaching campaign 🎯 the "right background" is a myth.
>
> The call's where it gets real. Our team will walk through your specific situation, what the first 90 days look like, and whether you'd be a fit. No hype, no pitch. Worth booking? 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- All three address the prospect's stated doubt ("possible for someone like me") head-on
- Option A names the specific archetypes who come from no-sales backgrounds (retail, hospitality, etc.), makes the prospect feel seen
- Option B is the most direct, reframes that talent doesn't matter, willingness does
- Option C uses a (real or representative) success story as social proof, useful for prospects who need evidence before they'll commit
- All three frame the call as where the real qualification happens, never as a sales pitch

## Example 3

**Setter input:**
> Prospect has shared: already closing, doing about $4k/month in commissions, wants to scale to $10k+, frustrated with current campaign quality.

### Option A (Match their level + bridge)

> [Name], you're not at the "should I do this?" stage. You're at the "which campaign is the right fit?" stage 🎯 different conversation.
>
> The call would get into the specific campaigns we have access to, the standards we hold our placed closers to, and whether you'd be the right fit for one of them. They'll also walk through how the programme accelerates your scaling beyond $10k.
>
> Want me to send the link? 😊

### Option B (Acknowledge the level + qualify upward)

> Got it [name] 👌 you've already proven you can sell. The question now is structural, better leads, better campaigns, better systems around your closing. That's where most $4k closers stay stuck for years.
>
> The call's the place to get into your specific campaign situation and what scaling to $10k+ actually looks like with our placement options. Worth booking? 😊

### Option C (Direct, fast bridge)

> [Name], at $4k/month you're past the "is closing for me?" stage. The next move is structural and the call's where we figure out which placement makes sense 🎯
>
> Mind if I send the link? 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- All three acknowledge the prospect's existing level, never talks down to a working closer
- Option A frames the prospect as past the "should I" stage, makes them feel respected, not pitched at
- Option B names the specific stuckness ($4k closers stay stuck), useful insight, then bridge
- Option C is the shortest, works for sharp, results-focused prospects who don't need warm-up
- All three frame the call as practical (specific campaigns, fit assessment), never as a sales pitch

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Always reference what they specifically shared.** Generic bridges fail. "Based on what you said about X" wins.
2. **Frame the call as a real conversation, not a pitch.** "Fit from both sides" / "no pitch" disarms the resistance.
3. **End with permission, not a hard close.** "Mind if I send the link?" / "Want me to set it up?", easy yes.
4. **Match the prospect's level.** Beginner = warmth + reframe. Working closer = directness + structural insight.
5. **Never name pricing.** The call is where pricing belongs.

## What to avoid

- "Let me hop on a call to show you why this is amazing!", guru speak
- Mentioning the price at this stage
- "If you're serious you'll book", manipulative
- Hard closes / pressure tactics
- Long restatements of the offer, they've heard it; bridge directly
- Saying "the call is free" defensively, implies it might not be
- Zero emojis (dead) or hype emojis 🔥 💯 🚀. 1-2 grounded emojis (✅ 🤝 😊 💡 🎯 👌) per message is the standard.
