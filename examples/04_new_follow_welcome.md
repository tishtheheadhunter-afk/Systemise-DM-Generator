# New Follow Welcome, Examples

**When to use:** Someone just followed Marco's account (Instagram, TikTok). We send a welcome DM to start a conversation early, before the relationship goes cold.

**Goal:** Friendly opener that doesn't feel automated, gives them a reason to reply.

**Generation count:** 2 options.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Generic new follower, no other context (we don't know what brought them in).

### Option A (Curiosity opener)

> Hey [name], welcome 👋
>
> Out of curiosity, what brought you here? I post a lot about high-ticket closing, building sales teams, and getting people placed as remote closers, so the audience usually falls into a few different camps.
>
> Knowing what you're here for helps me know what to send you.

### Option B (Light + low pressure)

> Hey [name], thanks for following 🙂
>
> Genuine question, what made you tap follow? Working in sales already, looking to break in, or just here for the content?
>
> Whatever the answer, no pitch. Just trying to understand who's in the audience 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- Both ask "what brought you here", gets the prospect to self-categorise, opens the door to qualifying
- Option A briefly mentions Marco's content range, useful for prospects who don't know what they're following yet
- Option B explicitly says "no pitch", disarms the auto-defensive response that "another DM means another sales pitch"
- Both end with a low-stakes question, easy to reply to
- 1-2 tasteful emojis per message (👋 🙂 😊 💡), placed at end of sentences, never stacked

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> New follower from a TikTok video about "the cost of inconsistent closing."

### Option A (Reference what likely brought them in)

> Hey [name], thanks for following 👋
>
> Out of curiosity, did the inconsistent closing video resonate? Most people who follow off that one are either closing already and frustrated with the swings, or trying to break in and seeing the gap.
>
> Which one's you?

### Option B (Reframe and ask)

> Hey [name] 👋
>
> The "inconsistent closing" thing is the most common pain I see 💡 most people think the issue is them, usually it's the lack of structure.
>
> Genuine question: are you closing already and trying to fix the swings, or trying to break in for the first time? 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- Both reference what likely brought them in (the inconsistent closing video), shows you're paying attention
- Both narrow the qualifying question to two clear options, easy to reply to
- Option A names the two common archetypes that follow that content, Marco voice (specific, observational)
- Option B reframes the pain ("it's not you, it's the structure"), Marco voice (insight-led)
- Neither pitches the programme. Conversation first, qualifier first, programme later.

---

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Reference what they followed off if you can.** "Did the [topic] video resonate?" beats generic welcomes.
2. **Ask what brought them in.** Self-categorisation is the fastest path to qualifying without it feeling like an interrogation.
3. **Disarm the "this is a pitch" assumption.** "No pitch" or "just trying to understand" softens the bar.
4. **Keep it short.** New follow welcomes that read like essays get ignored. 3-5 lines max.

## What to avoid

- "Welcome to the family!", cult-y
- Promotional offers in the first message, kills the relationship
- Long bios of who Marco is, they followed, they don't need a CV
- Emoji stacks or hype emojis (🔥 💯 🚀)
- Multiple questions in one message
- Greeting glued to the body without a blank line
