# Outbound DM Opener, Examples

**When to use:** We're reaching out to a prospect who hasn't messaged us first. Maybe they liked a post, commented something, or we're warm-prospecting from a list.

**Goal:** Get a reply. Open with relevance, not a pitch. Earn the right to ask the qualification question.

**Generation count:** 3 options.

**Formatting reminder:** Every option MUST have a blank line between the greeting and the next paragraph. Every option MUST include 1-3 emojis (placed at end of sentences/paragraphs, never at the start, never stacked). Outbound is colder, so emoji touch is light, never overdone.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Cold outreach to a prospect who liked Marco's recent post about high-ticket closing being a learnable skill.

### Option A (Reference their action)

> Hey [name], saw you liked the post on closing being a learnable skill 👋
>
> Quick question, are you looking at high-ticket closing for yourself, or curious from a different angle? 🎯
>
> Reason I ask, I run the training side of Systemise and Sell, and most people who like that post fall into one of two camps. Trying to break in, or already closing and trying to get more consistent. Wanted to see which one's you before I sent anything 😊

### Option B (Lead with insight)

> Hey [name], saw you engaged with the post 👋
>
> Quick thought 💡 most people who feel "stuck" in sales aren't bad at it. They've just never been properly trained on the actual structure of a high-ticket call. Discovery, problem amplification, presenting, objections, there's a process and most people are winging it.
>
> Curious where you're at with all of that. Closing already, working towards it, or just curious from a distance?

### Option C (Direct opening, no fluff)

> Hey [name] 👋
>
> Saw you liked the closing post. Are you a closer already or trying to break in? Asking because the answer changes what I'd send you 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- All three reference the specific post, the prospect knows this isn't blast outreach
- Option A introduces who Marco is briefly, frames the question as helpful triage
- Option B leads with a quick 💡 insight (the "structure not skill" reframe), earns attention before asking
- Option C is ultra-direct, works for prospects who clearly have low patience for chit-chat
- All three end with a qualifying question, not a pitch
- 👋 on the greeting is standard. The middle and end emojis are optional but ideal for warmth

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> Cold outreach to a prospect identified as "looks like a sales rep" from LinkedIn, has "BDR / SDR" in title at a tech company.

### Option A (Industry-aware)

> Hey [name], saw you're at [company] in BDR 👋
>
> Quick question, are you looking to stay in tech B2B or is the long-term goal to move into something where the OTE isn't capped by base + variable structure? 🎯
>
> Reason I ask, a lot of the closers we train come from BDR/SDR backgrounds and want the move to high-ticket commission-only where the upside is real. Wanted to see if that's where your head's at before I send anything 😊

### Option B (Career escape framing)

> Hey [name] 👋
>
> Quick honest question, are you looking to keep climbing the BDR/AE ladder, or is the goal to eventually break out of the corporate sales structure entirely?
>
> Asking because we run a high-ticket closer training programme and the people who do best in it are usually folks who've done the SDR grind, know they can sell, and want the next move to be on their own terms 🎯
>
> Wanted to know if that's relevant to you before sending more.

### Option C (Skip the interpretation, just qualify)

> Hey [name], saw you're in BDR 👋
>
> Are you happy where you are or eyeing a move? If you're eyeing a move, what's the direction? 😊

**Notes on what works:**

- All three reference what's specifically visible (BDR role), relevance over personalisation theatre
- Option A names the OTE / capped income pain point that BDRs feel, relevance + insight
- Option B frames the move as "on their own terms", speaks directly to the autonomy desire
- Option C is the shortest and most direct, fits prospects who would find longer messages performative
- All three avoid the BDR-spam clichés ("crushing it", "loved your profile"), Marco voice is direct, not sycophantic

## Example 3

**Setter input:**
> Outreach to someone who left a comment on Marco's post saying "I want to do this but I don't know if I'm cut out for sales."

### Option A (Reframe their self-doubt)

> Hey [name], saw your comment on the post 👋
>
> The "not cut out for sales" thing 💡 I hear that a lot, and almost every time it turns out to be a training problem, not a personality problem. The "naturals" you see online had to learn the same skills you'd be learning. They just had a head start.
>
> Quick question before I send anything, what's making you think you're not cut out for it? Past experience, or just the assumption? 😊

### Option B (Validate then redirect)

> Hey [name] 👋
>
> Saw the comment. Honest question, most people who say "I don't know if I'm cut out" are actually closer to it than they think. They just haven't had the structure or the reps yet ✅
>
> Before I tell you about anything, I'd rather understand where the doubt's coming from. Have you tried sales before, or is this the first time considering it?

### Option C (Direct challenge)

> Hey [name] 👋
>
> "Not cut out for sales" almost always means "haven't been trained yet." Sales is a skill, not a personality type. You either learn the structure or you don't 🎯
>
> Quick question, what's making you doubt yourself? If it's the structure stuff (discovery, objections, closing), that's all teachable. If it's the willingness to do reps, that's a different conversation.

**Notes on what works:**

- All three address the specific comment ("not cut out"), directly, not generically
- Option A reframes empathically with 💡: "you're not broken, you're untrained", Marco's signature reframe
- Option B validates first ("closer to it than you think") then qualifies
- Option C is the most challenging, only use when the prospect's tone suggests they can take it
- All three end with a question, not a pitch
- All three carry the core belief: sales is a skill, not a personality type. Voice consistency.

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Always blank line after the greeting.** Greeting on its own line, then a blank line, then the body.
2. **Reference something specific.** A post they liked, a comment they made, their job title, relevance over generic outreach
3. **Lead with insight or a reframe.** Earn the right to ask the question.
4. **Ask one question, not five.** First message is for getting a reply, not running diagnostics.
5. **Match the prospect's energy.** Direct prospect = direct outreach. Hesitant prospect = warmer outreach.
6. **Never pitch in the first message.** Never include programme details or pricing.
7. **1-2 emojis per outbound (lighter than inbound).** 👋 on the greeting is the workhorse. 🎯 / 💡 / 😊 / ✅ in the body if natural. Outbound is colder so do not pile them on.

## What to avoid

- Generic "Hi, hope you're well" openers
- Long sales pitches before they've engaged
- Hype emojis 🔥 💯 🚀 ⚡ or stacked emojis
- Asking too many questions
- Sounding like LinkedIn spam
- Sycophantic "loved your profile" / "your content is amazing", Marco doesn't flatter
- Greeting glued to body with no blank line
