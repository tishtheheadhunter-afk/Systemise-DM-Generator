# Comment Reply, Examples

**When to use:** Someone has commented on one of Marco's posts (or a Systemise and Sell post). We're replying publicly. The reply may be seen by hundreds of other people, so it doubles as content.

**Goal:** Engage genuinely, validate or challenge as appropriate, redirect interested commenters to DM keyword (CLOSER) or just continue the public conversation.

**Generation count:** 2 options.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Marco posted a video about high-ticket closing being a learnable skill. Comment from a prospect: "But isn't it true that some people are just naturally better at sales? My friend is a natural and I've never been able to close anyone."

### Option A (Public-facing reframe)

> Your friend isn't a "natural." They've just had more reps than you. Every "natural" closer I know was terrible on their first 50 calls 💡
>
> Sales is a skill. The reason it feels innate in some people is that they did the reps before they realised they were doing them. You can build the same thing, it just looks like training instead of accident ✅

### Option B (Reframe + soft DM CTA)

> Most "naturals" had a parent who sold, a first job that forced them onto the phone, or 3 years of door-to-door before anyone noticed. The reps were invisible 💡
>
> The skill is identical. The structure is teachable. Your friend just got a head start.
>
> If you want the breakdown of what the actual structure is, DM me CLOSER and I'll send the curriculum overview 💬

**Notes on what works:**

- Both publicly reframe the "natural" myth, content value for everyone reading the thread, not just the commenter
- Option A is pure value, no CTA, works when we want to build authority and trust public eyes
- Option B adds the DM CTA at the end, works when we want to convert engagement into private conversations
- Both validate the commenter's observation ("your friend is good") while reframing the cause ("they had reps")
- Neither attacks the commenter, that would damage Marco's brand publicly

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> Comment on Marco's post: "I've bought 3 closer courses already and none of them got me hired. Why is this any different?"

### Option A (Direct, addresses the skepticism)

> Fair question, and I'd be skeptical too in your position 👌
>
> Most "closer courses" are video libraries. You watch, you don't apply, nothing changes. We run weekly live training with me where we actually unpack scenarios, real roleplays with feedback, call breakdowns on actual campaigns, and guaranteed interview opportunities once you certify ✅
>
> It's not training plus hope. It's training plus access. If you've already done the courses and you're still not placed, the issue isn't more theory. It's getting structured reps and getting in front of hiring partners.

### Option B (Validate + redirect to private convo)

> Honest answer, I get it 👌 the closer training space is full of "buy a course and figure it out." That doesn't get anyone placed.
>
> The difference here is structural. Weekly live training, real roleplays, call breakdowns, and guaranteed interviews with vetted partners on completion ✅ training plus access, not training alone.
>
> If you want me to walk you through specifically why you didn't get placed from the previous courses and whether this would actually solve it, DM me CLOSER 💬

**Notes on what works:**

- Both validate the skepticism, never make a public commenter feel stupid for being skeptical
- Both name the specific gap in the previous courses (theory only, no application, no access to hiring partners)
- Both anchor the differentiation in concrete features (live training, roleplays, breakdowns, interviews)
- Option B redirects to DM for a private conversation about their specific situation, useful when the commenter is high-intent and the reply could continue
- Neither claim the previous courses were "scams", that's unprofessional and the public sees it

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Treat comment replies as public content.** They're not just for the commenter; they're for everyone reading.
2. **Validate before reframing.** Public attacks on commenters look bad even when the commenter is wrong.
3. **Add value first, CTA second.** The reply should be useful even if the commenter never DMs.
4. **DM CTA is optional.** Sometimes the right move is pure value with no CTA. Don't force it.
5. **Specific over generic.** Generic "great point!" replies are content noise. Reframes, insights, or specific answers are content gold.

## What to avoid

- Generic "great comment!" responses, useless content
- Defensive responses to skepticism, looks weak
- Long monologues, comment replies should be tight (2-3 short paragraphs max)
- Multiple DM keywords, pick one (CLOSER)
- Hype emojis (🔥 💯 🚀) or emoji stacks, not Marco voice. 1-2 tasteful emojis (👌 💡 ✅ 💬) is the standard.
