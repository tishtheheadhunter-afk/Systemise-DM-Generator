# Post Like Outreach, Examples

**When to use:** Someone liked a specific post but hasn't followed or commented. We're using the like as the opening signal to reach out.

**Goal:** Reference the specific post, ask one question, see if they're worth a longer conversation.

**Generation count:** 2 options.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Prospect liked Marco's post titled "The 3 reasons your closing is inconsistent."

### Option A (Reference + qualify)

> Hey [name], saw you liked the inconsistent closing post.
>
> Quick question, are you in the inconsistent closing camp yourself, or is it more theoretical for you?
>
> Reason I ask: I work with both, but the conversation looks different depending on which one. Wanted to know which lens to come from before I sent anything.

### Option B (Lead with insight)

> Hey [name], saw you engaged with the post on inconsistent closing.
>
> The thing most people miss in that post: the inconsistency isn't random. It's a feedback loop, bad reps create bad habits, bad habits create bad reps. Breaking the loop needs structured feedback, which most closers never get.
>
> Curious where you sit with all of this. Closing now and frustrated, or planning to start?

**Notes on what works:**

- Both reference the specific post directly, proves it's not blast outreach
- Option A asks the qualifying question fast, efficient, useful for high-volume outreach
- Option B leads with a quick insight ("inconsistency is a feedback loop") that adds value before asking, earns attention
- Both ask one question with two clear answer options, easy to reply to
- Neither pitches anything. The job of this message is to get a reply.

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> Prospect liked Marco's post about Marco's $200M sales career story.

### Option A (Reference Marco's story)

> Hey [name], saw you liked the $200M post.
>
> Out of curiosity, what made that one resonate? The career arc, the numbers, the systems angle?
>
> Knowing what hooked you tells me what to send you, if anything.

### Option B (Skip to qualifying)

> Hey [name].
>
> Saw you liked the $200M post. Quick question: are you in sales already, or thinking about getting into it?
>
> Different conversation depending on the answer, wanted to ask before sending anything generic.

**Notes on what works:**

- Both reference the specific post (the $200M story)
- Option A asks what specifically hooked them, useful for understanding their archetype before pitching anything
- Option B skips the "what hooked you" question and goes straight to qualifying, works when we're high-volume and need to filter fast
- Both end with permission to send something specific based on the answer

---

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Always reference the specific post.** "Saw you liked the [topic] post", generic outreach loses every time.
2. **Ask one question.** First-touch outreach should never feel like a survey.
3. **Either lead with insight or skip to qualifying.** Both work. Pick based on prospect heat.
4. **End with the qualifying question or permission to send.** Never pitch.

## What to avoid

- "Loved your engagement!", sycophantic
- Generic outreach that doesn't reference the actual post
- Multiple questions stacked
- Pitching the programme in the first message
- "Would love to hop on a call", way too fast, prospect hasn't even replied yet
