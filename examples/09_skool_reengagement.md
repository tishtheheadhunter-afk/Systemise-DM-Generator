# Skool Re-engagement, Examples

**When to use:** A Skool community member has hit qualification signals, completed Modules 1-3, attended 3+ daily lives in their first month, posted in chat, viewed the Hiring Partners course pages. We're surfacing the paid programme contextually, never as an upsell.

**Goal:** Acknowledge their engagement (validation), ask one qualifying question, frame the paid programme as the **next** step (separate from the community, not an upgrade).

**Generation count:** 2 options.

This is modelled directly on Tiago's Template 6, which has booked a lot of calls. The shape works.

---

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Skool member has completed Modules 1-3 within their first 14 days. They've also viewed the Hiring Partners course pages.

### Option A (Template 6 style, classic)

> Hey [name], saw you've worked through the first three modules, that's the foundations sorted.
>
> Quick question: are you closing already, or working towards it?
>
> Reason I ask: we run a separate placement programme for people who want to be working as a high-ticket closer for one of our partner companies. Guaranteed interview opportunities, 16-module curriculum, weekly live training with me. It's not for everyone, specifically for people ready to commit.
>
> If that sounds like you, happy to set up a call with our team to walk you through it. If not, no worries, keep working through the course.

### Option B (Slightly warmer, references Hiring Partners pages)

> Hey [name], noticed you've made it through the first three modules and had a look at the Hiring Partners course. Foundations are solid and you're already curious about the placement side.
>
> Quick question, are you closing already, or building up to it?
>
> The reason I ask: there's a separate paid programme that takes you from foundations to placed with one of our vetted partner companies. 16 modules, weekly live training with me, guaranteed interview opportunities. It's a real commitment though, so it's only for people genuinely ready.
>
> If you want to chat about whether it'd fit, happy to set up a call with our team. If not, no worries, keep going through the course.

**Notes on what works:**

- Both open by acknowledging specific engagement (Modules 1-3 done; Hiring Partners pages viewed), proves we're paying attention, not blast-DMing
- Both ask one qualifying question (closing already, or building up?), exactly the Tiago template
- Both frame the paid programme as **separate** from the community, not an upgrade
- Both explicitly say "not for everyone" / "real commitment", disqualifies tyre-kickers, attracts serious people
- Both end with permission to opt out gracefully ("no worries, keep working through the course"), never pressure

## Example 2

**Setter input:**
> Skool member has been actively posting in the chat for 2 weeks and attending 4-5 daily lives a week. They've completed Modules 1-5.

### Option A (Acknowledge engagement level)

> Hey [name], you've been showing up, Modules 1-5 done, posting in chat regularly, on most of the lives. That's the engagement profile we usually only see on people who are about to take the next step.
>
> Quick question: are you in the community to skill up for your own offer, or are you eyeing the placement track?
>
> The reason I ask is they're different conversations. If it's the placement track, we have a paid programme that gets you from where you are now to placed with one of our vetted partners. Worth a call with our team if that's where your head's at.
>
> If it's for your own offer instead, no problem, keep working through and let me know if you have specific questions.

### Option B (Direct + outcome-focused)

> Hey [name], you've been on most of the lives and you're 5 modules deep. That's serious engagement.
>
> Honest question: what's the actual goal here? Skilling up for your own thing, or building towards being placed as a closer for one of our partners?
>
> If it's placement, we have a paid programme that takes care of that, interviews guaranteed once you certify, 16 modules, weekly live with me. Different commitment than the free community. If you want to talk through whether it's the right fit, I can set up a call.
>
> If it's your own offer, all good, keep going.

**Notes on what works:**

- Both reference the specific engagement signals, modules done, lives attended, chat posts
- Both ask the **placement vs own-offer** qualifier, critical because the paid programme is specifically for placement-track people
- Both frame the paid programme as a "different commitment" from the free community, preserves the no-bait-and-switch positioning
- Both give a graceful opt-out ("keep going" / "all good"), protects the relationship even if they don't book

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Reference specific engagement signals.** "Modules 1-3 done" / "you've been on most of the lives", proves we're watching the right things, not spamming.
2. **Ask the qualifying question once.** Closing already vs working towards it. Placement vs own offer. One question, two options.
3. **Frame the paid programme as separate, not as an upgrade.** Critical for trust. The free community has zero upsell inside it, and our messaging has to match that.
4. **Disqualify gently.** "Not for everyone" / "real commitment", the prospect should feel that saying no is fine.
5. **Easy opt-out.** "No worries, keep going through the course", never punishes a no.

## What to avoid

- "Looks like you're ready for the next level", patronising
- "Now that you've seen the value of the free stuff, here's the paid one", feels like a bait-and-switch
- Multiple qualifying questions in one message
- Pressure language ("limited spots", "promotion ends Friday"), destroys the trust we just built
- Long offer pitches before the prospect has agreed to a call
- Pricing in any form
