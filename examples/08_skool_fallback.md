# Skool Fallback, Examples

**When to use:** The prospect has declined the discovery call **on grounds of investment, money, or timing**. Examples that should trigger this response type:

- "I can't afford that right now"
- "The investment isn't possible for me at the moment"
- "I'm interested but money's tight"
- "I'd love to but I'm not in a position to pay yet"
- "Maybe later when I have the funds"
- "I'd need to save up first"

**When NOT to use:** Any other type of decline. "Not interested", "wrong fit", "this isn't for me", those get a clean exit, not Skool. See `context/04_the_closing_room_skool.md`.

**Goal:** Acknowledge the no without arguing. Pivot to the free Skool community as a no-strings way to audition the training. Make it crystal clear it's free with no upsell pressure.

**Generation count:** 3 options.

This is the heaviest-lifting response type in the whole bot. Get it right and we save lost leads. Get it wrong and we sound desperate.

## Example 1

**Setter input:**
> Prospect just said: "Look, I'd love to do this but I genuinely can't afford it right now. Maybe in a few months."

### Option A (Direct pivot, no pressure)

> Totally hear you [name]. Don't want to push you into something the timing's wrong on 🙂
>
> One thing worth knowing, we run a free community called The Closing Room. Same first two-thirds of the training, daily live sessions with me Mon-Fri, no charge, no upsell inside. People use it to audition the methodology before any paid commitment.
>
> If you want the link, here it is, https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> If not, all good, door's always open down the line.

### Option B (Reframe the wait)

> No worries [name], honest answer is the timing has to be right 🙂
>
> While you're saving up, you could come into our free community, The Closing Room. Daily lives with me, the actual training course (free), and you can take your time working through the foundations before any paid step. Most people spend 30-60 days in there before they're ready for a call.
>
> If you want in, https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> No pressure either way. If the few months turn into the right time, you'll know where to find me.

### Option C (Acknowledge + offer + exit)

> Got it [name], I respect that 🙂
>
> If you want to start now without spending anything, we have a free Skool community where the course content is open and I run daily lives Mon to Fri. No upsell inside, no pressure to upgrade, you can take your time and decide later.
>
> Link if useful, https://community.systemiseandsell.com/join
>
> Either way, when you're ready, hit me back.

**Notes on what works:**

- All three open by acknowledging the no without arguing. **Never** "I get it but…"
- All three explicitly say "free", "no upsell", "no pressure", disarms the assumption that this is a downsell trap
- All three give the link inline, easy to copy, easy to act on
- All three end with "door's open" / "either way" framing, the prospect leaves feeling respected, not chased
- Option A is the cleanest default
- Option B reframes the wait productively ("while you're saving up, use the time")
- Option C is shortest, works for prospects who clearly want a quick exit

## Common patterns to notice

1. **Acknowledge the no first.** "Got it", "Totally hear you", "Makes sense", never "but", never "I understand but".
2. **Position Skool as no-pressure, no-upsell, free.** Say it explicitly.
3. **Frame Skool as productive, not consolation.** They're not "settling for less"; they're using the time strategically.
4. **Drop the link inline.** No "DM me for the link", that adds a step, creates friction.
5. **Exit gracefully.** "Door's open / when you're ready", never "I'll follow up next week" (that's pressure dressed up as helpfulness).

## What to avoid

- "I get it, but…", arguing with the no
- "If you really wanted this you'd find a way", manipulative, damages brand
- "Limited spots in the free community", false scarcity, kills trust
- "You'll regret not jumping in now", pressure tactic
- Implying Skool is a downsell or "second-best" option, it's a separate, legitimate path
- "Once you're in Skool we can talk about upgrading", wrong framing; Skool is its own thing

## The structural reason this works

Skool is the save mechanism. A prospect who declines on price isn't a lost lead; they're a future paid customer who needs warming. The fallback message gets them into a free environment where:

1. They take the course (free, real value)
2. They engage with Marco's daily lives (build trust over time)
3. They engage with the community (peer pressure / social proof)
4. Eventually they hit qualification signals (Modules 1-3 done, Hiring Partners pages viewed, etc.)
5. Tiago (or the setter) re-approaches with the Skool Re-engagement template
6. Booked call, closer to a yes than they were the first time

The Skool fallback is the entry point to a 30-90 day warming cycle. Mess it up and we lose them clean. Get it right and the prospect comes back warmer than they left.
